Veterans With Lupus Skin Symptoms May Qualify for a Clinical Study

If you have experienced lupus-related rashes, lesions, sores, or scarring, you may qualify for a dermatology research study evaluating a new treatment for cutaneous lupus.

Takes under 2 minutes

Participants may receive:

  • $115 per visit compensation
  • Up to $50 travel + $50 meal reimbursement
  • No-cost dermatology exams and lab testing
  • Potential access to a new lupus treatment
  • Opportunity to continue treatment (up to 2 years)

Why Veterans Are Being Contacted

At Thank You For Your Service, we periodically review our veteran records to identify opportunities that may benefit members of our community.

During that process, we noticed you may have previously mentioned lupus or lupus-related skin symptoms.

Because of that, we wanted to make you aware of a clinical research study being conducted by a dermatology research team.

This study is designed for individuals with cutaneous lupus, a form of lupus that affects the skin.

What Is Cutaneous Lupus?

Cutaneous lupus erythematosus (CLE) is an autoimmune disease that affects the skin. Symptoms can be persistent and difficult to manage with standard care.

Persistent rashes
Sores or lesions
Scarring
Discoloration
Hair loss
Skin inflammation

Many people continue to experience these symptoms even after trying common treatments like hydroxychloroquine (Plaquenil).

New Treatment Evaluation

This research study is evaluating a medication designed to help reduce inflammation and disease activity in people living with lupus skin conditions.

Investigational Study

The study focuses on the safety and effectiveness of a new dermatological approach.

Dermatology Experts

Care provided by specialists familiar with complex autoimmune skin conditions.

Participants who complete the first year may have the opportunity to join a 2-year long-term extension study.

Continue receiving study medication at no cost for up to 2 additional years.

What Participation Looks Like

A clear, structured path from eligibility to treatment

1

1. Screening Appointment

The research team will review medical history, skin symptoms, and prior treatments. A skin biopsy may be performed to confirm diagnosis.

2

2. Study Treatment Phase

Participants receive either the investigational medication or a placebo initially. Later, all participants transition to the study medication.

3

3. Monitoring Visits

Regular visits where doctors monitor your skin symptoms, overall health, and laboratory results to ensure safety and track progress.

Who May Qualify

18 years of age or older
Diagnosed with cutaneous or discoid lupus
Currently have rashes or lesions
Symptoms have not improved with standard care
* Final eligibility is confirmed by the study doctor during your screening visit.
