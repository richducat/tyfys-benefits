How TYFYS fits into your VA claim

We don’t replace the VA or an accredited representative. We focus on evidence, so when you’re ready to file, you’re not guessing what to send—or scrambling for records at the last minute.

1. Intake & goals

Share your service history, current rating, major conditions, and what you’re trying to accomplish. Your TYFYS advocate uses this to build your roadmap.

2. Records & questionnaires

Track down your DD‑214, service treatment records, and private medical records, then complete short “mini‑DBQs” and condition‑specific questionnaires to guide your providers.

3. Private provider evaluations

TYFYS coordinates private medical evaluations focused on VA rating criteria—covering primary and secondary conditions tied to your MOS, era, and deployment history.

4. Final evidence packet & filing

Once your documents are in, we help you assemble a clear, VA‑ready evidence packet mapped to the forms you’ll use. You then file directly with the VA or an accredited representative.

Is TYFYS the right fit for you?

TYFYS can be a good fit if…
  • You already have a VA rating but believe it’s too low.
  • You’re filing for multiple conditions and don’t want to miss secondary issues.
  • Your MOS, deployments, or era came with unique exposures.
  • You want private medical opinions and detailed DBQs—not just a quick C&P exam.
You should know…
  • You can always file a VA disability claim on your own or with a free accredited VSO. TYFYS is optional support.
  • We do not provide legal advice and do not represent you before the VA or any court.
  • We cannot promise a specific rating, effective date, or outcome. Those decisions are made only by the VA.
  • Our pricing is a flat‑fee model, agreed up front, with no percentage of your backpay taken.
