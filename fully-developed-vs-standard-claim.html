Fully Developed Claims vs. Standard Claims: Speed Up Your Decision

When you file for disability, you have two lanes: the Slow Lane (Standard Claim) and the Fast Lane (Fully Developed Claim). Unfortunately, most veterans unknowingly choose the slow lane.

2. Fully Developed Claims (FDC): The Cheat Code for Faster Decisions

Topic: Process Speed | Reading Time: 5 Mins | Keywords: FDC, VA Backlog, Claim Speed

The VA currently has a backlog of over 300,000 claims. The average wait time for a standard claim can be 120-150 days or more. However, there is a "fast lane" called the Fully Developed Claim (FDC) program.

Standard vs. Fully Developed: The Metaphor

❌ The Standard Claim (Slow Lane)

You tell the VA: "I'm hurt. Go find my records."

The VA must then legally send letters to the DoD, private doctors, and archives. They wait 30-60 days for responses. They schedule exams. They gather the hay before they can find the needle.

✅ The FDC (Fast Lane)

You tell the VA: "Here is the proof. Make a decision."

You submit everything upfront: Private DBQs, Nexus Letters, Service Records, and Buddy Statements. The VA skips the "gathering" phase and goes straight to "rating."

Why Everyone Doesn't Do This

Most veterans (and overworked VSOs) don't have access to private doctors who know how to fill out DBQs correctly. They have to use the Standard lane because they need the VA to provide the exam.

Our Advantage: Because we provide the medical evidence upfront, we exclusively file Fully Developed Claims for our clients. This often allows us to bypass the C&P exam entirely, putting the control back in your hands.
