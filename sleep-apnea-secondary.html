12. Sleep Apnea as a Secondary Condition: How to Prove It

Topic: Sleep Claims | Reading Time: 5 Mins | Keywords: Sleep Apnea VA Claim, Secondary to PTSD, CPAP Rating

Sleep Apnea is a high-value claim (50% if you use a CPAP). However, it is very hard to prove as a "direct" service connection if you weren't diagnosed with a sleep study while on active duty.

The "Secondary" Strategy

The winning strategy for most veterans is Secondary Connection. We help veterans provide medical nexus letters linking Sleep Apnea to conditions they are already rated for:

  • Secondary to PTSD: PTSD can aggravate Sleep Apnea through stress and sleep disruption.
  • Secondary to Sinusitis/Rhinitis: Breathing issues from burn pits can cause apnea.
  • Secondary to Weight Gain: If an orthopedic injury (like bad knees) caused you to gain weight (obesity), and that weight gain caused Sleep Apnea, it can be service-connected as an "intermediate step."

This medical logic requires a strong Nexus Letter to connect the dots, which is exactly what we provide.
