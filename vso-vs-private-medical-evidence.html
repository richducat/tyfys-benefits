VSO vs. Private Medical Evidence: What's the Difference?

There is a common misconception that you should "never pay for help" with a VA claim because VSOs (Veterans Service Organizations) are free. While VSOs do incredible work, it is important to understand their limitations.

3. VSO vs. Private Medical Evidence: What's the Difference?

Topic: Service Comparison | Reading Time: 5 Mins | Keywords: VSO, DAV, Private Claims Agent, Medical Evidence

We are often asked: "Why should I pay for help when a VSO is free?" It is a valid question. We respect VSOs and the work they do. However, there is a critical difference between "filing a claim" and "building a winning case."

The VSO Role: Administrative Support

VSOs (Veterans Service Organizations like the DAV, VFW, or county reps) are excellent at administrative tasks. They have access to the VA's VBMS system. They can see your claim status and help you fill out the 21-526EZ form.

The Limitation: VSOs are not doctors. They cannot write medical opinions. If you go to a VSO with back pain but no recent diagnosis or Nexus Letter, they will likely just submit the claim "as is" and hope the VA schedules an exam. This is the "Standard Claim" path, which has a high denial rate for complex cases.

The Private Evidence Role: Medical Support

Our service focuses on the medical evidence gap. We are not just form-fillers. We are evidence builders.

  • We audit your file: We look for the "High Value" claims a VSO might miss because they are too busy.
  • We connect you with doctors: We have a network of private providers who understand VA disability law (38 CFR).
  • We create the proof: Our doctors write the DBQs and Nexus Letters that legally prove your disability exists and is service-connected.

When to Use Which?

Use a Free VSO If... +

You have a very straightforward claim (e.g., you were shot in combat and have the medical records). You just need someone to push the "submit" button and don't need new medical evidence.

Use Private Evidence (Us) If... +

You have been denied before. You have a complex case (secondary conditions). You don't have a current diagnosis in your VA file. You want to speed up the process with an FDC.
