What is the "Blue Button"?

It is a universal symbol for "Download My Data". When you click it on VA.gov, it generates a single PDF or Text file containing your selected medical history.

Why Veterans Use It:

  • • Sharing blood work trends with a private cardiologist.
  • • Keeping a backup copy for emergencies.
  • • Reviewing doctor's notes (Clinical Notes) for accuracy.

The Process

3 Steps to Download

1

Sign in to VA.gov

Use your Login.gov or ID.me account. Navigate to the "Health Care" section.

2

Select "Blue Button Report"

Look for the link that says "VA Blue Button Report" (often under "Health Records"). It may redirect you to the My HealtheVet portal.

3

Customize & Download

Crucial Step: You must select a Date Range (e.g., "Last 3 Months") and check the boxes for the data you want (e.g., "VA Lab Results"). Then click Download PDF.

Why It Matters

Instant Access vs. The "Old Way"

Why fill out forms when you can click a button?

Average time to receive records.

The "Release of Information" Trap

Traditionally, getting records required filling out VA Form 10-5345 and waiting for the "Release of Information" office to mail you a packet.

When to use the Old Way:

Only use the formal request if you need Certified records for a legal case. For medical care, the Blue Button PDF is usually sufficient.

Method Comparison

Which Method is Right for You?

Blue Button (DIY)

Best for doctor visits, personal tracking, and quick reference. Fast, free, and immediate.

Official Request (ROI)

Best for lawyers or court cases where a "Certified" stamp is legally required.
