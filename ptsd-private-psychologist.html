6. PTSD Claims: Why You Need a Private Psych

Topic: Mental Health | Reading Time: 6 Mins | Keywords: VA PTSD Rating, Private Psych Eval, Mental Health DBQ

Mental health claims are uniquely challenging because they are subjective. Unlike a broken bone, you can't see PTSD on an X-ray. This makes the C&P exam extremely risky if you are relying on a VA-contracted doctor.

The 15-Minute Exam Problem

We consistently hear from veterans that their VA C&P exam for PTSD lasted less than 20 minutes. The examiner asked a few generic questions, checked some boxes, and rushed them out the door. How can a stranger understand your trauma, your nightmares, and your social anxiety in 15 minutes?

This rush often leads to a rating of 30% or a flat-out denial, even if your symptoms warrant 70% or 100%.

The Private Difference

When you work with us, we connect you with a private psychologist or psychiatrist via Telehealth. They take the time to:

  • Listen to your full story and history.
  • Understand how your condition affects your occupational and social impairment (the key phrase for higher ratings).
  • Complete a comprehensive DBQ that accurately reflects the severity of your symptoms.

This detailed report forces the VA rater to consider the full picture, not just a 15-minute snapshot.
