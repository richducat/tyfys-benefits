The Two Types of 100%

The VA is confusing. There are two very different ways to get paid at the 100% rate ($3,737/mo for a single vet in 2025).

Option A: Scheduler 100% (The Goal) NO LIMITS

How you get it: Your disabilities add up to 95% or higher using VA math (e.g., 70% PTSD + 50% Apnea + 40% Back).

How you get it: Your rating is less than 100% (e.g., 70%), but you apply for "Total Disability Individual Unemployability" because you cannot work.

Work Rules: You are capped at the federal poverty level (approx $15k/year). If you earn more, you lose the benefit.

5. Increasing Back Pain Ratings: It's About Motion, Not Pain

Topic: Physical Claims | Reading Time: 5 Mins | Keywords: 38 CFR 4.71a, Range of Motion, Spine Rating

If you tell the VA "my back hurts level 10," they might give you 10%. Why? Because the VA rates the spine (Cervical and Thoracolumbar) based on Range of Motion (ROM) mechanics, not subjective pain.

The "DeLuca" Factor (The Secret Weapon)

There is a court case called DeLuca v. Brown. It states that the VA must rate you not just on your standard motion, but on your motion during a flare-up or after repetitive use.

If you can touch your toes once, but after 5 times your back locks up at 30 degrees due to pain, you legally deserve the 40% rating. VA C&P examiners often skip the "repetitive use" test. Many veterans get stuck at 80% or 90%. They keep trying to increase their primary ratings (like knees or back) but hit a ceiling because their range of motion isn't "bad enough." The secret to breaking through to 100% is often Secondary Conditions.

9. The 5-Year Rule: When is Your Rating Protected?

Topic: Rating Protection | Reading Time: 4 Mins | Keywords: VA 5 Year Rule, Rating Protection, 10 Year Rule, 20 Year Rule

Veterans often worry: "If I file for an increase, will the VA reduce my current rating?" It's a valid fear, but there are specific rules that protect your rating over time.

The 3 Key Protection Rules

The 5-Year Rule +

If you have held a rating for 5 years or more, the VA cannot reduce it based on a single exam. They must prove "sustained improvement" over time (usually requiring multiple exams showing you got better). This makes your rating much safer.

The 10-Year Rule +

Once you've been service-connected for a disability for 10 years, the VA cannot sever the "service connection" unless they can prove fraud. They can still reduce the percentage (if you improve), but they can't take away the connection.

The 20-Year Rule +

This is the gold standard. If you have held a rating for 20 years continuously, the VA cannot reduce it below its original level, even if your condition improves in the future.

17. VA Math Explained: Why 50% + 50% Doesn't Equal 100%

Topic: Ratings Math | Reading Time: 3 Mins | Keywords: VA Disability Calculator, Combined Rating Table, VA Math

The VA uses a concept called the "Whole Person Theory." If you are 50% disabled, you are 50% "healthy." The next rating only applies to the "healthy" portion that remains.

So, 50% + 50% = 75% (which rounds up to 80%). Getting from 90% to 95% (which rounds to 100%) is the hardest leap in the entire system. This is why you need a strategy to identify high-value claims (50%+) rather than piling on tiny 10% ratings.
